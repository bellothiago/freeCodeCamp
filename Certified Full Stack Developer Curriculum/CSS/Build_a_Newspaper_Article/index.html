City Adopts Bold Green Transit Plan

Council approves five-year program to expand bike lanes and reduce car trips

Jonas Lonas

The city council voted unanimously Monday to adopt a comprehensive transit plan aimed at cutting single-occupancy vehicle trips by 25 percent over five years. The initiative includes a rapid rollout of protected bike lanes, priority bus corridors, and incentives for shared mobility services.

Supporters say the program will improve air quality, lower commute times, and make streets safer for pedestrians and cyclists. Opponents expressed concern about short-term parking loss and requested a phased review after the first 18 months.
